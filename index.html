Post-2020 lifestyle and Interests

The whole world came to a standstill with the outbreak of an outrageous virus, Coronavirus Disease, aka Covid-19. People went into lockdown, enclosing themselves under a single roof, for an unknown period of time. Phrases, like work-from-home and online classes, started going viral and became the new normal. The contagion of technology spread apace with the virus. Now, students are attending classes virtually sitting at home, lying on their beds, or in any other comfortable position. Who wouldn’t have wanted that as a student?

Life before the New-Normal

In pre-lockdown times, students had to greet the day early before heading out for school or university. However, presently educational institutes reside just a click away, simultaneously requiring no proper attire for attendance. Who knew the fantasy of every student would someday come true? Students can spend endless time with their families and focus on their non-work-related interests, like every day is a holiday.

Experiences

The Unrealized Reality

lifestyle altered

However, this newfangled lifestyle wears off fairly quickly due to the compromises to these merits. Students can’t go outside to play, even if they have finished all their homework. They are caged inside their house, all day, every day. Lack of interaction is causing the students to become more and more reticent. What’s more, it has become harder for them to keep up with their routines. Most of the students can’t wake up early without a morning lecture driving them out of bed. This has caused variations in both their sleep and eating patterns. Such irregularities develop anxiety, mental health issues arise which exasperates learners and reduces their concentration power. Not to forget, the fear of the virus already has everyone staggered and depressed.

Future is Uncertain

Being grateful for what we had

But, it won’t be like this forever. This pandemic will end someday and things will go back to normal. Till then, students will have to stay positive and focus on the things they can control. To overcome anxiety and depression, they can meditate or exercise daily which helps to maintain physical as well as mental well-being. They can learn new skills or try new recipes which will help in regaining their focus. Students can still go out for a walk around the neighborhood, breathe in fresh air whilst ensuring safety precautions and social distance. This might relieve them from the constant stress of staying indoors.
